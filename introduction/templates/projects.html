  • WordPress

    April 2016

    I had an interview for a software engineering internship that summer. It did not work out, however, the experience of interviewing and seeing how much more I have to learn was great. After that experience, I decided to learn WordPress, so I can focus on working with smaller clients who are willing to pay for websites.

    I learned hard lessons about WordPress. When some people see that you are a new coder who needs to make money. They will take advantage. I made the mistake several times because I wanted to show my good faith, and my appreciation that people trusted me to build their website. I would start building a website & then would not be compensated or would start my work without a signed contract in my hands. If I can give other intermediate coders any advice : never do that, even if you need the money. Make them sign, if they say that they were put off by your request, be absolutely certain that they had no attention to pay you in the first place. I know some of them are so good and persuasive, or worse, they are working for a cause that is important to you, don't do it. Have respect for your intellectual property & your work.
